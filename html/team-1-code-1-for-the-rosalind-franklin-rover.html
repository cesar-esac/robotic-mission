    STEP 4: READ LINE-FOLLOWING SENSOR VALUES FROM THE ROVER

    4.1 If the line-following reading is 1 on both sensors (left and right) and we check that the line width is less than 1.5 cm.

    NOTE: For the first tests, set half of the indicated speed values, as the test track is positioned at a certain height.


    STEP 5: ACTIVATE MOTORS IN RESPONSE TO LINE FOLLOWING

    Remember: in Maqueen

    • 0 = black (line detected) and 1 = white (no line detected).
    • M1: left motor and M2: right motor.

    5.1 If both sensors do not detect the line, it may be because the line is thinner and the robot is moving inside the line while following it.

    If (left sensor = 1) and (right sensor = 1) neither sensor detects the line Maqueen motors: both motors forward, speed = 100 / 4 = 25.


    5.2 If the left sensor detects the line and the right sensor does not, the rover must turn left to follow the line. To do this, the right motor moves forward and the left motor stops.

    Once both sensors detect the line (0), the rover should continue moving forward.

    If (left sensor = 0) and (right sensor = 1) the left sensor detects the line and the right sensor does not the rover is drifting to the right, so it must turn left Maqueen motors:

    • Right motor: forward at speed = 100 / 4 = 25

    • Left motor: backward at speed = 100 / 2 = 50 / 4 = 12

       

    5.3 If the left sensor does not detect the line and the right sensor does, the rover must turn right to follow the line. To do this, the left motor moves forward and the right motor stops.

    The left sensor does not detect the line and the right sensor does the rover is drifting to the left, so it must turn right Maqueen motors:

    • Right motor: forward at speed = 100 / 2 = 50 / 4 = 12

    • Left motor: backward at speed = 100 / 4 = 25

    7.5 If a sensor leaves the line, the rover corrects its direction towards the opposite side.
